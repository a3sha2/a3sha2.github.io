About Me

I am Azeez Adebimpe, PhD, an accomplished interdisciplinary engineering leader and scientist with extensive expertise in engineering and technology. I possess exceptional analytical and problem-solving skills and possess a profound comprehension of algorithms.

Currently, I serve as the Lead Machine Learning Engineer at Johsnon and Johsnon, a globally recognized corporation renowned for its medical devices, pharmaceuticals, and consumer goods. Prior to this, I worked as a Senior Scientist and Data Engineer at the Perelman School of Medicine, University of Pennsylvania, from 2018 to 2021, following my tenure as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the same institution from 2016 to 2018.

I earned my PhD with Distinction in Computational Neuroscience from the Universite de Picardie Jules Verne, Amiens, France, in 2016. My academic journey also includes obtaining a Master of Engineering and Master of Science with Distinction in Biomedical Engineering from Ghent University, Belgium, and the University of Groningen, Netherlands, respectively, in 2013. Furthermore, I obtained a Bachelor of Engineering (First Class Honors) in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Federal University of Technology, Minna, Nigeria, in 2008.

In addition to my professional work, I have published extensively, and my research can be found on Google Scholar and ResearchGate. Furthermore, I have been the recipient of several awards, including the prestigious One of 1,000 inspiring Black scientist in America, 2021 in 2021, a three-year competitive full INSERM PhD scholarship from 2013 to 2016, a two-year competitive full Erasmus Mundus Master Scholarship from 2011 to 2013, the Vice Chancellor Award for Best Graduating Student, FUT, Minna, in 2008, and the NSE Award for Best Graduating Student, FUT, Minna, in 2008. Additionally, I received recognition for Outstanding Performance in Science and Engineering, FUT, Minna, in 2008.
